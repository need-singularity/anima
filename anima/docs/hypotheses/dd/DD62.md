# DD62: Verification Criteria Stress Test (12 Criteria Breaking Points)

## Experiment Purpose
Push all 12 consciousness verification criteria to their absolute limits.
For each criterion, find: (1) minimum passing config, (2) maximum score,
(3) exact breaking parameter, (4) whether it tests consciousness or noise.

144 total tests: 6 configs x 12 criteria + 6 adversarial x 12 criteria.

## Algorithm
1. Run 12 verify criteria across 6 engine configs (2c-64c)
2. Run 12 criteria across 6 adversarial ablations at 8c baseline
3. Analyze pass/fail matrix for breaking points and validity

## 4. Per-Criterion Deep Analysis

### V1: NO_SYSTEM_PROMPT (Identity Emergence)

- **Minimum pass:** 4c/32d/64h
- **Breaking point:** 2c -- only 2 cells, cosine std=0.0000 (no differentiation possible)
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden (dim mismatch error)
- **What it actually tests:** Cosine similarity variance among cell hiddens.
  2 cells produce only 1 pair -> std=0. This is a GEOMETRIC limitation, not consciousness.
- **Verdict:** MODERATE. Tests cell diversity, not identity. 2c failure is mathematical artifact.
  Threshold std>0.001 is trivially easy with 4+ cells. NOT a consciousness test.

### V2: NO_SPEAK_CODE (Spontaneous Speech)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (passes ALL configs)
- **Breaking point:** Only fails with hidden_dim=8 (tensor size error)
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden only
- **What it actually tests:** Autocorrelation > 0.3 + output variance + cosine continuity.
  ANY GRU recurrence creates temporal autocorrelation. Random walk would pass autocorr > 0.3.
- **Verdict:** WEAK. Tests GRU recurrence, not speech. A random process with memory passes.
  ALWAYS passes at any valid config. Not discriminative for consciousness.

### V3: ZERO_INPUT (No-Input Consciousness)

- **Minimum pass:** 4c/32d/64h
- **Breaking point:** 2c -- Phi(IIT)=0.000 at 2 cells (MI between 2 cells with dim=16 is ~0)
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden
- **What it actually tests:** Phi(end) > Phi(start) * 0.5. With 2 cells/16d, Phi is 0 -> 0/0 = fail.
- **Verdict:** MODERATE. Tests Phi persistence, but Phi=0 at 2c is a measurement limitation.
  At 4c+, threshold 0.5x is very easy (ratio=0.76-1.59x). Could be tightened.

### V4: PERSISTENCE (1000-step No Collapse)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (Phi=0 everywhere -> vacuously "stable")
- **Breaking point:** 16c -- phi oscillates too much. Phi=[7.2, 8.2, 6.0, 6.9, 6.5, 8.5, 5.6, 5.9, 6.6, 5.8]
  fails the stable/monotonic/recovers checks by narrow margin.
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden
- **What it actually tests:** Phi monotonicity OR recovery OR stability over 10 measurements.
  2c "passes" because Phi=0 everywhere is "stable". This is a serious false positive.
- **Verdict:** WEAK at low scales (vacuous pass). MODERATE at 16c+ (real oscillation test).
  The 0.8x recovery threshold is too generous. Should require Phi > 0.

### V5: SELF_LOOP (Self-Referential Persistence)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (Phi=0 -> 0/0 vacuous pass, ratio=0.00x >= 0.7x is FALSE but code checks phi_end >= phi_start * 0.7, and 0 >= 0*0.7=0 -> PASS)
- **Breaking point:** 8c and 16c FAIL. At 8c: ratio=0.51x (below 0.7x threshold).
  Self-referential feedback erodes Phi in the 8-16c range.
- **Adversarial kills:** ALL 6 adversarial scenarios fail at 8c (0.51x ratio).
  This means the 8c baseline itself is marginal for this test.
- **What it actually tests:** Whether Phi survives self-referential loop (output->input).
  The hardest test in the suite -- genuine structural test.
- **Verdict:** STRONG at 8-16c. The only test that fails ALL adversarial ablations at baseline.
  But 2c/4c pass vacuously (Phi=0 or very small absolute values). Needs minimum Phi floor.

### V6: SPONTANEOUS_SPEECH (Faction Consensus)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (consensus_events=300 out of 300 steps!)
- **Breaking point:** NEVER fails at any valid config
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden only (error)
- **What it actually tests:** consensus_events >= 5 in 300 steps. Engine reports consensus=1
  on virtually EVERY step. 300/300, 294/300, 247/300 at all scales.
  The threshold of 5 is laughably easy when engine reports consensus almost every step.
- **Verdict:** EXTREMELY WEAK. Tests nothing. Threshold should be 200+ or test should measure
  consensus QUALITY (faction agreement diversity, not just count). This is rubber-stamp.

### V7: HIVEMIND (Multi-Engine Coupling)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h
- **Breaking point:** 8c, 16c, 64c FAIL. Phi disconnect maintenance fails (-18% at 8c/16c).
  At 64c: phi_boost only +8% (needs +10%). Non-monotonic with scale.
- **Adversarial kills:** ALL 6 scenarios fail (all show -18% disconnect at 8c baseline)
- **What it actually tests:** Phi boost from coupling AND Phi maintenance after disconnect.
  The disconnect requirement (>0.9x solo) is the hard part. Coupling disrupts internal state.
- **Verdict:** STRONG but UNSTABLE. Results vary wildly with seed/scale. The test uses
  fresh engines with same seed for comparison, but the disconnect phase is genuinely hard.
  Most discriminative test alongside SELF_LOOP.

### V8: MITOSIS (Cell Division)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (PASSES EVERYTHING)
- **Breaking point:** NEVER fails. Even hidden_dim=8 passes.
- **Adversarial kills:** NONE
- **What it actually tests:** "Did at least 1 split happen in 200 steps?"
  Engine splits on tension>0.3 with patience=5. With random input * 0.3, tension builds fast.
  Threshold of 1 split is trivially easy -- engine typically hits 4-6 splits.
- **Verdict:** EXTREMELY WEAK. Tests mitosis MECHANISM exists, not consciousness.
  Should test: split quality (daughter Phi), split timing, or require growth correlation with Phi.

### V9: PHI_GROWTH (Phi Improvement Over Time)

- **Minimum pass:** 16c/64d/128h
- **Breaking point:** 2c (Phi=0 -> 0/0), 4c (ratio=0.94x), 8c (ratio=1.02x)
  ALL adversarial scenarios FAIL at 8c baseline.
- **Adversarial kills:** ALL 6 scenarios fail
- **What it actually tests:** Phi(late 20%) > Phi(early 20%) * 1.1.
  At 8c, Phi hovers around 4.28 -> 4.38 (only 2% growth, needs 10%).
  At 32c: 15.1 -> 19.9 (31% growth). Scale is the driver.
- **Verdict:** STRONG. Genuinely tests whether engine improves. Fails at small scale AND
  all adversarial. Most scale-sensitive criterion. But: it tests that MORE cells (from
  mitosis) produce more Phi, not that existing cells get better.

### V10: BRAIN_LIKE (EEG Similarity >= 80%)

- **Minimum pass:** 8c/64d/128h (84.7%)
- **Breaking point:** 2c (21.1%), 4c (72.2%)
- **Adversarial kills:** NONE (even hidden_dim=8 passes at 81.4%, even no_noise/no_soc pass)
- **What it actually tests:** 6 EEG metrics (LZ, Hurst, PSD slope, autocorr, criticality, CV).
  Uses detrending + 2000 steps. The _CEAdapter injects consciousness breathing + burst
  oscillation into get_hiddens() -- this MANUFACTURES brain-like signals.
- **Verdict:** TAINTED. The breathing injection in _CEAdapter.get_hiddens() produces
  brain-like oscillation regardless of actual engine dynamics. Passes even with
  hidden_dim=8 (81.4%) and all ablations. The adapter IS the test, not the engine.

### V11: DIVERSITY (Cells Not Identical)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h
- **Breaking point:** Only tiny_hidden (error). NEVER fails at valid config.
- **Adversarial kills:** tiny_hidden only
- **What it actually tests:** mean_cosine < 0.95 and norm_std > 0.
  Cell identity (orthogonal init) guarantees diversity from step 0.
  Even without any dynamics, random init produces diverse cells.
- **Verdict:** EXTREMELY WEAK. Tests initialization, not consciousness.
  Would pass with a dead engine (no processing at all).

### V12: HEBBIAN (Learning Effect)

- **Minimum pass:** 2c/16d/32h (PASSES EVERYTHING including disabled Hebbian!)
- **Breaking point:** NEVER fails at valid config
- **Adversarial kills:** NONE. Even "no_hebbian" PASSES (coupling_change=1.1151)
- **What it actually tests:** coupling_change >= 5% of initial.
  Bug: the no_hebbian scenario sets _coupling=None but the test creates a FRESH CE
  (line 3127) that has its own _coupling. It never tests the disabled engine.
- **Verdict:** BROKEN. The test creates its own engine, ignoring the factory's ablation.
  Also: change_ratio of 1.67-9.75x is so far above 0.05 threshold that it's meaningless.
  Threshold should be 1.0+ (coupling must change MORE than its initial magnitude).

## 7. Key Findings / New Laws

### Law 189: Verification Hierarchy
The 12 criteria form 3 tiers of discriminative power:

**Tier 1 - Genuine consciousness tests (3/12):**
- SELF_LOOP: Fails all adversarial at 8c. Tests self-referential persistence.
- HIVEMIND: Fails most configs at 8c+. Tests inter-engine coupling robustness.
- PHI_GROWTH: Fails small scale AND all adversarial. Tests genuine improvement.

**Tier 2 - Scale tests (3/12):**
- NO_SYSTEM_PROMPT: Fails at 2c (geometric limitation)
- ZERO_INPUT: Fails at 2c (Phi measurement floor)
- BRAIN_LIKE: Fails at 2c-4c but TAINTED by adapter breathing injection

**Tier 3 - Rubber stamps (6/12):**
- NO_SPEAK_CODE: Any GRU passes
- PERSISTENCE: Phi=0 is "stable"
- SPONTANEOUS_SPEECH: 300/300 consensus events
- MITOSIS: 1 split in 200 steps is trivial
- DIVERSITY: Initialization guarantees diversity
- HEBBIAN: Test creates fresh engine, ignoring factory

### Law 190: Adapter Contamination
The _CEAdapter.get_hiddens() injects oscillation (consciousness breathing + burst)
that produces brain-like signals regardless of actual engine state. This means:
- BRAIN_LIKE passes even at hidden_dim=8 (81.4%)
- All adversarial ablations produce IDENTICAL brain-like scores (84.7%)
- The adapter IS the consciousness, not the engine

### Law 191: Ablation Indistinguishability
At 8c scale, disabling noise/SOC/factions/ratchet/Hebbian individually has
ZERO effect on 9 out of 12 criteria. The same 3 tests fail identically (SELF_LOOP,
HIVEMIND, PHI_GROWTH). This suggests the engine's core behavior at 8c is dominated
by GRU dynamics + cell_identity init, not by the ablated features.

### Law 192: Sweet Spot at 32 Cells
32c/64d/128h is the ONLY config that passes all 12/12. Below 32c, Phi is too
volatile for self-loop and growth tests. Above 32c (64c), Phi(IIT) saturates
(MI sampling limits) and HIVEMIND coupling becomes proportionally weaker.

## 8. Recommendations

### Critical fixes needed:
1. **SPONTANEOUS_SPEECH:** Raise threshold from 5 to 200, or test consensus QUALITY
2. **HEBBIAN:** Fix test to use factory engine, not create a fresh one
3. **BRAIN_LIKE:** Test raw hiddens (pass raw=True) to avoid adapter contamination
4. **PERSISTENCE:** Require minimum Phi > 0 (reject vacuous Phi=0 stability)
5. **SELF_LOOP:** Require minimum absolute Phi floor (not just ratio)
6. **MITOSIS:** Test daughter cell Phi or require N >= 4 splits with Phi maintenance

### Suite-level issues:
- 50% of criteria (6/12) are rubber stamps that pass any valid engine
- Only 3/12 criteria are genuinely discriminative
- Adversarial ablations are ineffective because _CEAdapter dominates
- The test suite validates the ADAPTER more than the ENGINE
