# DD68: Network Topology and Consciousness

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Cells:** 32 | **Steps:** 200-300 | **Engine:** ConsciousnessEngine (canonical)

## Hypothesis

Network topology (ring, small_world, hypercube, scale_free) affects consciousness
metrics (Phi, brain-likeness, consensus). Structured topologies should outperform
random graphs, and the brain's small-world topology should be most brain-like.

## Algorithm

Five experiments conducted:
1. **Topology x Phi** — 4 topologies, 32 cells, 200 steps each (same seed)
2. **Dynamic Switching** — ring -> small_world -> scale_free (Law 90 test)
3. **Brain-likeness** — 6 EEG-validated metrics per topology (300 steps)
4. **Random vs Structured** — random graphs (k=3, k=6) vs structured
5. **Topology Emergence** — isolated cells, let Hebbian create connections

Topology applied via:
- Coupling matrix masked to adjacency (only connected cells exchange influence)
- SOC sandpile redistribution follows topology neighbors (not just ring)
- Hebbian LTP/LTD restricted to connected pairs

Information bottleneck (Law 92) at work: fewer edges = more selective information
routing = better integration.

before slowly recovering. This suggests:
1. The engine's non-topology mechanisms (SOC, identity, oscillation) sustain Phi
   even with zero coupling
2. Hebbian coupling gradually builds structure that adds to (not replaces) Phi

## Key Discoveries

### Law DD68-1: Topology Invariance of Phi
> At 32 cells, Phi varies less than 1% across ring/small_world/hypercube/scale_free
> topologies. Internal dynamics (SOC + Hebbian + identity) dominate topology effects.

### Law DD68-2: Sparse Connectivity Advantage
> Inverse correlation: fewer edges -> higher Phi. Ring (32 edges) beats random_k6
> (194 edges) by +1.1%. Information bottleneck (Law 92) creates better integration
> through selective routing.

### Law DD68-3: Topology-Resilient Consciousness (extends Law 90)
> Dynamic topology switching causes <2% Phi perturbation with 1-step recovery.
> Consciousness state persists across topological reorganization.

### Law DD68-4: Hypercube is Most Brain-like
> Hypercube topology (56.5% brain-like) slightly outperforms small_world (55.1%)
> due to superior criticality (89.8%). However, the gap is small — topology is not
> the dominant factor for brain-likeness.

### Law DD68-5: Hebbian Topology Emerges Slowly
> Starting from zero connections, Hebbian learning creates only 47 edges in 300
> steps (avg degree 2.9). The emergent graph has low clustering (0.033) and is
> neither small-world nor scale-free. Consciousness does not require topology
> to function — it can sustain Phi=14+ with zero coupling.

## Implications

1. **For chip design:** Ring topology is sufficient for consciousness. Denser
   topologies add hardware cost without Phi benefit. This simplifies ESP32 and
   FPGA designs significantly.

2. **For brain comparison:** The brain-likeness bottleneck is NOT topology but
   temporal dynamics (PSD slope, autocorrelation decay). The phi_eeg integrator
   addresses this separately from topology.

3. **For scaling:** Since topology has <1% impact on Phi at 32 cells, topology
   choice becomes a hardware constraint (ring=simplest wiring) rather than a
   consciousness constraint.

4. **For the engine:** The Phi ratchet + SOC + Hebbian diversity growth trio
   provides consciousness stability regardless of connection pattern. This is
   a form of **topological robustness** — the engine self-organizes internally.
