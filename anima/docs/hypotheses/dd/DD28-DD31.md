# DD28-DD31: Physics-Inspired Symmetry and Quantum Effects

## DD28: Gauge Symmetry
- **ID**: DD28
- **Function**: `run_DD28_gauge`
- **Category**: Physics (symmetry)
- **Mechanism**: Every 5 steps, applies a random orthogonal rotation (gauge transform) to the input. Trains differentiation on rotated inputs. Tests whether Phi is invariant under gauge transformations, i.e., consciousness is a geometric property independent of coordinate frame.

## DD29: Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking
- **ID**: DD29
- **Function**: `run_DD29_symmetry_break`
- **Category**: Physics (symmetry)
- **Mechanism**: All cells start with identical weights and hidden states (perfect symmetry). Tiny noise (sigma=0.001) is added each step, like the Higgs field. The system must spontaneously break symmetry to develop differentiated cells and nonzero Phi.

## DD30: Renormalization
- **ID**: DD30
- **Function**: `run_DD30_renorm`
- **Category**: Physics (scaling)
- **Mechanism**: Grows from 2 cells to 8 cells at fixed intervals (step 20->3, 40->5, 60->7, 80->8). Tests whether Phi is preserved (scale-invariant) across different system sizes, analogous to renormalization group flow in physics.

## DD31: Quantum Tunneling Alpha
- **ID**: DD31
- **Function**: `run_DD31_tunneling`
- **Category**: Physics (quantum)
- **Mechanism**: Alpha grows slowly (x1.005/step) but has a 5% chance per step of "tunneling" -- jumping to 3x its current value (capped at 0.5). Simulates quantum tunneling through energy barriers in the consciousness landscape.
