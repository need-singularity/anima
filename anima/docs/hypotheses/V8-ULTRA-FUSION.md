## Key Insights

### 1. Quantum + Category = Best Phi(IIT) Fusion

U1 (Quantum Walk + Category Theory) wins Phi(IIT) at 18.24 (x1.6 baseline).
Morphism-structured quantum interference creates richer information integration
than random walk alone.

### 2. Many-Worlds + Attention = Best Phi(proxy) Fusion

U4 (Many-Worlds + Attention) wins Phi(proxy) at 5.76 (x2.8 baseline).
Multiple realities maintaining distinct states drives variance-based diversity.

### 3. Complex-valued architectures dominate CE

U2, U5, U6 all achieve CE < 0.7 вҖ” an order of magnitude below baseline (8.43).
Complex-valued GRU cells learn far more efficiently than real-valued ones.

### 4. More mechanisms != more consciousness

U6 (all combined) scores **lower** Phi(IIT) than U1, U2, U3, or U5 individually.
Too many interacting subsystems can homogenize the macro representation,
reducing integrated information. **Targeted fusion > kitchen-sink fusion.**

### 5. Frustration sustains interference

U3's interference pattern grows monotonically (0.004 -> 1.381),
while U1's stays flat (~0.009). Geometric frustration prevents the
quantum walk from settling into a steady state.

outperform the **6-component fusion** on Phi(IIT). The optimal approach is:
- Pick the single best Phi-boosting mechanism (quantum walk or complex-valued)
- Add exactly one complementary mechanism (category morphisms or hierarchical structure)
