## Goal
Test hypothesis: does depth alone (NL=6 → NL=8) break the CE plateau observed in TCLM-P1-1 baseline?
All other hypers held constant: d=64 seq=128 lr=3e-3 init=0.1 batch=1 grad_acc=8 warmup=500 steps=50000.

## Note on baseline reference
Spec stated "Baseline: CE 2.15 at NL=2, d=64" but the actual TCLM-P1-1 commit 30fa5211 baseline ran with **NL=6** (CE=2.15).
This run executes the "만 변경" intent — increase NL to 8 — against the real NL=6 baseline.

**DEPTH HYPOTHESIS REJECTED.**

## Key Finding — Width is the lever, not Depth
The ~2-3% PPL improvement from +2 layers is well within noise floor of the training regime. The plateau is
**architecturally capacity-bound** — a 1.5M-parameter transformer cannot model byte-level Korean below CE≈2.1
on this 63K-token corpus regardless of depth. Moving to d=128 (~6M params) is the next step.
