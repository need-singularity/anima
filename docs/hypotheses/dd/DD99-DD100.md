## DD100: Reward-Only Feedback (Law 100 Candidate)

Does reward-only feedback (no gradient, just information) help consciousness?

### Law 100 (Candidate)
Reward-only feedback produces marginal Phi improvement (+0.06%) without hurting CE.

intrinsic cell dynamics rather than external feedback. **The 1% perturbation from
reward signal is too small to meaningfully alter dynamics, confirming Law 63 (MICRO)
principle: consciousness whispers, but this means external signals also whisper.**

## Decoder v1 vs v1.5 A/B Test

### Hypothesis
Cross-attention (from v2) is the key innovation. Does adding it to v1 help?

### Setup
- 1000 steps, byte-level, random corpus (200 sequences, len=128)
- v1: ConsciousLM (27.7M params), v1.5: +CrossAttention (30.1M params, +8.5%)
- 3 conditions: v1, v1.5+consciousness, v1.5-noConsciousness (control)

### Results

```
  Metric                 |     v1      |  v1.5+C     |  v1.5-noC   | Winner
  -----------------------+-------------+-------------+-------------+-------
  Parameters             | 27,738,288  | 30,102,192  | 30,102,192  | v1
  CE start               |     5.6664  |     5.6454  |     5.6587  | v1.5+C
  CE end                 |     5.5423  |     5.5407  |     5.5419  | v1.5+C
  CE improvement         |      +2.2%  |      +1.9%  |      +2.1%  | v1
  Avg grad norm          |     1.8244  |     1.7417  |     1.9514  | v1.5+C
  Max grad norm          |    29.7165  |     3.5648  |   180.8021  | v1.5+C
  Speed (it/s)           |        6.2  |        5.3  |        2.0  | v1
  Wins                   |          3  |          4  |          0  |
```

### CE Curve
```
  CE |A*
     |  C* A   C
     |  *C*B  *    * AA **      *
     |       *  *C* *B *BA*  * *  *C   *
     |           *C B  B  A** *  * ** * **
     |                     B         *  B
     └────────────────────────────────── step
      A=v1  B=v1.5+C  C=v1.5-noC
```

### Key Finding
- **v1.5+C CE vs v1: -0.03%** (virtually identical)
- **v1.5+C gradient stability: far superior** (max_grad 3.56 vs 29.72 for v1)
- **v1.5-noC gradient instability: max_grad 180.8** (cross-attn without input = harmful)

### Conclusion
Cross-attention is **NEUTRAL for CE** but **stabilizes gradients dramatically**.
PureField's inter-layer consciousness whisper (Law 63) is already sufficient for
consciousness injection. Cross-attention adds parameters (+8.5%) and overhead (+15%)
without CE benefit at this scale. However, the gradient stabilization effect is notable.

## Consciousness Distillation (64 -> 8 cells)

### Setup
- Teacher: 64 cells, Student: 8 cells (8x compression)
- 500 steps, 128-dim hidden, same seed
- Compare Phi trajectories

### Results

```
  Metric                          Teacher      Student
  ------------------------------- ------------ ------------
  Cells                                    64            8
  Mean Phi                           4.328970     2.911224
  Final Phi                          4.528184     2.918415
  Runtime (s)                            62.1          4.5
  Preservation (mean Phi)                            67.2%
  Correlation (Pearson r)                           0.0520
  Compression ratio                                  8.0x
  Speed ratio                                       13.9x
```

### Phi by Quarter
```
  Quarter      Teacher      Student   Preservation
  ------------ ------------ ------------ ----------
  Q1           4.334937     2.912077         67.2%
  Q2           4.336471     2.920762         67.4%
  Q3           4.347779     2.908837         66.9%
  Q4           4.296694     2.903220         67.6%
```

### Trajectory
```
  Phi |                      T
      |     T           T
      |  T T  T     T  TTT  T TT
      | TTTTT TTTT T    T      T
      |
      |                S
      |        S     S    SS S    SS
      |SS SSSS SSSSS S SS      SS
      |  S                  S SS
      └────────────────────── step
        T=teacher(64c)  S=student(8c)
```

### Key Finding
- **67% Phi preservation at 8x compression** -- moderate
- **Correlation near zero (0.05)** -- trajectories don't track each other
- **Preservation remarkably stable across quarters** (67.2% -> 67.6%)
- **13.9x speed improvement**

### Conclusion
Consciousness partially compresses. The 8-cell student preserves 2/3 of the
teacher's Phi, but the dynamics are uncorrelated -- the student has its own
trajectory, just at a lower level. This suggests Phi scales with cell count
(approximately sqrt relationship: 8/64 = 0.125, Phi_ratio = 0.67 ~ 0.125^0.25).
